# US-12.6 - Support Ticketing System

**Epic:** Admin & Audit Controls  
**Phase:** Phase 12 - Admin & Audit  
**Status:** Defined (New)  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Complexity:** Medium  

## User Story

As a **Customer Support Agent**,  
I want a centralized ticketing portal to manage user-raised inquiries and issues,  
so that I can provide professional, asynchronous help and maintain a history of user resolutions.

## Acceptance Criteria

### 1. Unified Ticketing Inbox
- [ ] **Inbox View**: Paginated list of all active tickets with filters for `Priority` (P1-P4), `Category` (Wallet, KYC, Technical, Trade Dispute), and `Status`.
- [ ] **State Machine**: Tickets must follow a standard lifecycle: `OPEN` → `IN_PROGRESS` → `RESOLVED` → `CLOSED`.

### 2. Communication & Collaboration
- [ ] **Asynchronous Comments**: Support can reply to tickets with internal-only notes or user-facing comments.
- [ ] **File Attachment Support**: Support can view user-uploaded screenshots or documents related to their issue. (Note: No live chat in this phase).

### 3. Service Level Agreement (SLA) Tracking
- [ ] **Time-to-Response**: Track the time between ticket creation and first Support response.
- [ ] **Auto-Escalation**: P1/P2 tickets that are not responded to within 4 hours must be visually highlighted and escalated to the Operations role dashboard.

### 4. User-Specific History
- [ ] **Contextual Sidebar**: When viewing a ticket, display the user's basic profile, recent wallet adjustments, and last 5 orders for immediate context.

## Technical Design Considerations
- Tickets should be stored in a dedicated `support_tickets` collection, linked to `user_id`.
- Communication history must be immutable and audit-logged.
- Future-proof the data model to support WebSocket-based live chat in later phases.

## Business Analyst Insights
> [!NOTE]
> The ticketing system is the primary "Customer Voice" of the platform. Response times and resolution quality directly impact platform trust and user retention.
